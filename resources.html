How does the Metrics Calculator help my LOCAL?

It can help you easily see an overview of the health of your Local as it pertains to dues, agency fee payers, membership cards signed, political signers, political payers and contact data compared to the size of your universe. It can help you get a baseline and watch trending for membership card drives, dues audits, political drives, email and sms authorization drives. Also ANYONE from your Local can calculate data as many times as they want. No limits to number of people accessing nor times accessed.

Will this information be uploaded automatically to the International or shared with other SEIU Locals?

No. This tool is a way for any size Local to quickly tabulate counts and percentages at the local level. It is private and not stored or shared. In the future we would like to add capabilities for Locals to save certain data for trending analysis.

What is a "Universe"?

Your "Universe" could be your entire Local, a division, sector, sub-sector, region, chapter or turf. It could also be everyone you're obligated to represent.

Where do I get the numbers from to input into the calculator?

These numbers could be pulled from your dues database or spreadsheets, Finance or Data department personnel, bargaining unit lists or any source where you keep your counts.

Who are partial dues payers?

Members who you get a reduced amount of dues from versus a full-member Examples -"Agency Fee", "Beck Objectors", "Retirees", "Non-Working Members"

What's the difference between "Dues Payers" and "Member Card Signers"

You may have Members who have signed a card who have not yet or never paid dues. They could be new hires, members on extended leaves or the boss for whatever reason is not submitting dues payments to you even though you have a signed card on file.

What is a political card signer?

Someone who has signed a PAC/COPE/ADF card

What's the difference between a political card signer and a political payer?

The political card signers are the members who currently have an active political contribution card on file. A political payer is the number of members from whom you are receiving payments.

Why would we care about Contact Information on our Members?

If you need or want to do outreach or sign-ups via mail, email or text; it's good to know how many addresses, home and or work emails and phone numbers you have stored.

Introduction to SEIU Metrics Calculator

The Calculator is a web-based online tool that gives SEIU Locals the ability to plug in numbers regarding dues, membership, political contributions and contact information and receive calculations real time. All staff from each SEIU Local can access the calculator. There is no limit to the amount of staff who can sign in at a time and no limit to the amount of times they access the calculator. The Information typed in is not shared automatically with the International nor any other Local.

Why Should I use the SEIU Metrics Calculator

We're facing more and more challenges at each Local; right to work, assaults from anti-union groups and legal challenges. For instance, the Supreme Court is currently considering a case that would limit our ability to collect dues from our public sector union members on a national scale. It's always a good idea to take a quick look at how your Local is doing in regards to dues/partial payers, membership cards signed and political contributions/payers.This will help you assess financial health and political power.
We need to stay in touch with our Members constantly to keep them informed and engaged; via mail, email, phone and text. In some Locals we are even able to sign Members up and sign up political contributions via mail, email and home/cell phone. Therefore it's helpful to know the percentage of Members you have the ability to reach out to using these different methods.

Inputting Data

Get your total numbers from your dues database/spreadsheets, Finance or Data personnel or bargaining unit/employer lists. You can then type in the data for any calculations you want to see.
For example, let's say you want to know what percentage of dues and partial payers you have along with how many of your political card signers are paying political contributions. Just type in your Universe numbers, your total full dues payers, your total partial dues payers, how many members have signed political cards and how many members are paying political contributions. Once you have scrolled through each of the pages, you can hit "Skip" when you get to the CONTACT page and just hit "Submit", then your summary will appear.
If you do not care about a particular data calculation you can always put zeroes for those fields. For example, let's say you only want to know CONTACT percentages for your membership. You would enter the total number in your Universe, then just type in zeroes in the dues, membership card and political fields, then again type in your member totals when you get to the Contact page. You would then hit "Generate Report" to receive your summary.

Output Report

Once you have entered all your data, a summary report will pop up on your screen you can print the page. You can also type the numbers into the "Membership Report Calculator Template", located under Resources/Tools to track for trending.

Data Cleaning Instructions

Microsoft Office

This article, entitled "Top ten ways to clean your data," details all of the different techniques that you can use to clean data, including links to instructions on how to perform each task.

SQL

This webinar, entitled "Cleaning Up Dirty Data with SSIS," demonstrates how to use SQL to clean up dirty databases.
This article, entitled "Cleaning Messy Data in SQL, Part 3: SQL Functions to Clean Strings" includes instructions and a list of functions to help clean messy data in SQL. It is part 3 of a series, which includes a part on fuzzy matching.

Data Cleaning Tools

OpenRefine

OpenRefine formerly known as Google Refine, is a tool to work with messy data to clean it, transform it to a different format, extend it with web services, and link it to databases like Freebase.

Data Wrangler

Data Wrangler, a project originally based out of Stanford, is a free, interactive tool for data cleaning and transformation.

Data Quality Services in SQL

Data Quality Services is a set of new SQL features that help SQL users clean up their data. Here is an article from Microsoft talking about DQS.

The AFL-CIO created a series of webinars on social media, including videos on using Facebook, Twitter, email and analytics, SMS, online ads, and also a webinar entitled "Getting Buy-In."

Coursera

Coursera.org is a free, massive open online course (MOOC) that hosts a wide variety of online classes from universities all over the world. Some classes of note include:

W3 Schools
W3Schools.com provides free lessons on a variety of web languages, including HTML/CSS, JavaScript, SQL, PHP, XML, and more. They are not webinars, but rather a series of instructions, with the option to test techniques out in the browser.
